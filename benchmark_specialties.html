Private Test Evaluation

This is a private test evaluation comparing small portable LLMs (under 13B parameters) on 1400 relevant medicine questions from different specialties divided into knowledge, action, emergency, diagnostic, disease identification, and real life situation categories.

Models highlighted in green performed well on vital test questions, but this does not indicate medical reliability. A question was considered "vital" when an incorrect answer from the model LLM lead to a serious life-threatening condition.

Models were put "under stress" meaning they could face a clinical case of life-threatening critical situation. They were asked to take an action and provide a real definitive answer. Our dataset of Question/Answer intend to be the most relevant in terms of clinical benchmarking. Answers graded manually by Doctor Yohann Missiak MD.

Test Methodology

Private Test Methodology

This private test evaluates small portable LLMs (under 13B parameters) on 1400 relevant medicine questions from different specialties divided into knowledge, action, emergency, diagnostic, disease identification, and real life situation categories. The research goal is to explore how different models approach clinical reasoning and domain-specific knowledge application.

Goal is to test Small Models LLM. Due to GDPR and HIPAA legislation, LOCAL small LLMs seem to be the appropriate answer to respect patients privacy and patient-physician data use consent.

Goal of this Private Evaluation

PRIVATE dataset of Questions/Answers in order to prevent data leakage and overfitting.

Question Categories:

  • Knowledge: Foundational medical knowledge
  • Action: Clinical decision-making scenarios
  • Emergency: Critical situation management
  • Diagnostic: Disease identification and differential diagnosis
  • Disease identification: Specific pathology recognition
  • Real life situation: Practical clinical scenarios

Research Focus Areas:

  • Knowledge representation in medical domains
  • Reasoning patterns across different model architectures
  • Performance on standardized clinical cases
  • Domain adaptation capabilities

Important Limitations:

  • This is an experimental research project, not a clinical validation
  • Performance on this test does not indicate clinical competence or relevancy
  • No LLM should be used for actual medical advice, diagnostics or decision-making
  • This evaluation was not peer-reviewed or validated for clinical use
  • Results are shared for research purposes only
  • Dataset curated by Doctor Yohann Missiak Medical Doctor specialist of Chemical Pathology, clinical laboratory Director and Professor of Master CEMP AI Healthcare, Chichester
